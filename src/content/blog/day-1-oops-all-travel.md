## Welcome to the start of this trip!

Hey! First of all just wanted to welcome you all to this website I made in about a few hours! Today marks the official start of Benji and I's trip to Thailand. Unfortunately, despite it being the official start, we won't be in Thailand till July 14th(for everyone in the US) and July 15th for us in Thailand. Regardless, Just thought it would be nice to give a little Day 1 plan and a general plan for our trip.

# Whats the Immediate Travel Plan?

By 8:18 pm CDT, We will be off on our first flight to LA. we will land there roughly 10:55 pm PDT(UTC-8), so about 4 and a half hours in the air.

Our next flight is an hour after, to Hong Kong, and that flight will (painfully) be nearly **15 hours**, departing at 11:55pm PDT and arriving at 5:40 am HKT(UTC+8). This is the flight that will either kill me and Benji, or make us closer. My plan for this flight is to replay Pikmin 4, seeing how far I can get or start reading the book "Snow Crash" by Neal Stephenson. As for Benji, he also packed a book, but plans on trying to sleep.

Finally, we have Hong Kong to Bangkok, which starts departs at 7:40 am, and lands at 9:35 am ICT(UTC+7). This would be an easier flight in comparison and gives us a little break after our long flight. From here we will be in Bangkok, and we will check into a Hostel for the night before going to Hat Yai the next morning!

# So Whats the Overall Plan?

So overall, from the southern most city of Hat Yai, we plan to spend a day there, a couple of days in Trang, a day in Krabi, some time in Phuket, a couple of nights in Ayutthaya, finally reaching the north of Chiang Mai, then flying back to Bangkok for the remainder of the trip. All of our stays are organized in a spreadsheet, and for the most part we are travelling by bus and train!

# My Thoughts before the trip

Being completely honest, I am very nervous, but so excited. Its a big trip, to a part of the world I have never experienced. I am not that familiar with the language, and I'll be gone for a good amount of time. Despite that(or maybe because), I am so excited. I am excited to experience the culture, the food, meet the people, and get a broader view of Thailand than most people get to see, and I just feel so excited to have the opportunity to see it all, from South all the way to the North. That's all I really have to say pre-trip. I look forward to hearing all of your messages :)\
