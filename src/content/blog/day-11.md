Hey yall! Once again doing this later than I wanted, but both these past 2 days I have been  too wiped to stay up past midnight writing. Today was a big physical activity day, so my energy was GONE.

# Quick Morning

Benji and I walked to this place I had saw that sold “Kaya Toast”, a common thailand breakfast specialty that uses a coconut jam on toast. Went to this place called Xorin, has a cute vibe and gave us a nice light breakfast.

We didn’t give much time to breakfast or relaxing this morning as we went straight from breakfast to preparing for our day. We planned to hike up to the big budha. We were told there is a trail from the base of Karon in Phuket to the top of the mountain. Its less commonly done than just taking a taxi up, but our logic was that it would be more rewarding.

To get there, there was a bus in town that went from old town to patong, and it could drop us off nearby, one left every 30 minutes roughly. After doing a quick 7/11 trip for water, we hopped on, and went to the base of the mountain

# The Hike

Getting to the trail was easy from the bus, we walked up past a couple markets(in which benji got some chicken skewers from), and then started the upward climb.

At first it was just short off the road, but quickly we ascended into more and more unstructured pathing. To the extend we were unsure we were on the right path at times, but we were. We came to the conclusion that during the rainy season the trail is probably not well maintained so it gets to be a little harder.

![](../../assets/img_2259.jpeg)

For me, I just had a lot of energy that day, so I was able to climb and jump around and I was having a grand old time with it. Benji on the other hand was feeling a bit off, so the hike was a bit harder at times. Regardless, we took breaks for each other, and so that we could keep the energy up till the top.

walking around we got to see various types of fauna and flora, everything from snakes hanging of cables and ropes to large groups of bamboo. The unstructured nature of the hike gave it a feeling of exploration.

![](../../assets/img_2261.jpeg)

Eventually, we reached some road, and this was the final bit of pathing before the budha, climbing this, was the easy part.

# The Big Budha

We could easily see this from miles away, but upon getting closer it was astonishing to see the amount of details. A bit of a side tangent, but both Benji and I are astonished by not only the engineering of budha’s like these, but also like fact that logistically, making them seems extremely challenging, getting things up on top of mountains.

The Big Budha had a little plaza setup around it, where you could see monkeys just going on about there day. From here we could get an entirely different view of phuket from what we got before on our first hike here. This mountain being much taller, and being across the range allowed us to see the west side of phuket a lot easier.

Upon walking up the stairs, we finally reached the Budha base, On the outside you can see monks interacting with tourists, as well as other little statues surrounding the base. Upon going inside, the budha was nearly hollow with primarily just support beams in there. I don’t have much to say besides it was marvelous.

![](../../assets/img_2275.jpeg)

Upon walking down, before leaving, we decided to reward ourselves with a 60 baht coconut, and it felt so refreshing to drink it all up and eat the insides of.

Upon exiting, it started to rain and drizzle, and while we like hiking, it would be a tad dangerous to do it in the rain given the conditions we saw, so we called a Grab. Now if you plan to take Grab, be prepared because its on the more challenging side to get one here, took us 20 minutes opposed to the usual 5.

# Wat Chalong

Our next destination was Phuket’s most famous temple, Wat Chalong. It was famous for good reason: Out of all the temples we had been to, this one has been the largest in area.

There were about 4 different buildings we had explored. 

The first one was smaller on the inside and primarily just had 3 statues in which locals came to pray to.

The second one we went to was more quaint building containing monk statues.

![](../../assets/img_2293.jpeg)

The third building was a room containing just the budha as well as people studying in there(as well as some cats).

The final building was the one that took most of our time though. This was the main temple and had 3 floors. The first one contained various statues of the budha. The second floor contained more, but with one stone budha that was reminiscent of the big budha we saw earlier. Then the final floor, lead us outside and then into a tower where one could make a wish. Benji decided to make a wish there!

![](../../assets/img_2292.jpeg)

Overall, it was such a beautiful temple, and despite seeing plenty other temples, this one stood on its own well.

# The Evening: Getting Back and Enjoying the Night

We decided on the way back given grab was getting expensive to take the bus back. Now, warning to those who take the bus here, it will not be on time, not within 5 minutes of expected departure or 10 either, it will be a gamble. We ended up just sitting there for 30 minutes. Eventually an English lady had come to the bus stop and started a conversation with us, she asked if we thought that she had time to get a soda from across the street, and we said “yeah probably”, and the second she left and went into the 7/11, we saw the bus. Benji and i stalled the bus for a minute as she ran back across the street, and all 3 of us ended up catching it.

On the way back, we ended up chatting with her more, apparently, she had been living here for the past 7 years, just living a free spirit kind of life style, and it was interesting to learn about her and her life experience in Thailand. She had been an english and science teacher here, and she gave us a lot of insight into the education culture, how tourist scams work. We ended up teaching her about the current state of the UK given she had not kept up at all with it or UK politics, which if you know anything about British politics over the past decade, it has been interesting to say the least.

We said good bye to our new friend in Old town, and Benji and I quickly made a realization: we were hungry and had not had lunch. We didn't want to spoil dinner, so we made the executive decision to get snacks at 7/11 and munch on those. I ended up eating a mackerel onigiri, a thai iced tea, and chips. While both American 7/11 and Thai 7/11 are very similar, Benji and I have grown to see why there is so many 7/11s in Thailand, because truly feel convenient.

After resting for a bit, we decided to go our to dinner at this fancier Thai-Chinese Resteraunt called “The Charm”. There we had gotten a few things, an oolong tea, a salad to share, a whole grilled fish, whatever the locals caught, and some spicy beef, and the fish was probably the most flavorful fish I have had here, it was so fresh and savory.

![](../../assets/img_2316.jpeg)

While that meal was amazing, we decided we had gotten comfortable with the stuff locals went to, after being in more local towns, so for dessert we wandered till we found this little restaurant that served us fried dough and pandan, and it felt like the best way to end the night. Today more than most felt very adventurous for Benji and I, and we were excited to fit all of these last few things in before we headed off from phuket in 2 days. This next day ahead of us seems to be more rainy, so I am glad we got our exploration fill today.
