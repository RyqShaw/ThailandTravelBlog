Hey Yall! Today was another slower day, Benji’s stomach was feeling off today so our original plans we have decided to post pone to another day, but we still ended up doing plenty anyway!

# Khao Rang Viewpoint

We got breakfast at this place called Cafe Delight, a western style breakfast restaurant, where I got shrimp eggs Benedict and benji got a French toast, nice and simple. Here we just talked about our plans for the next few days given Phuket is the first place we stay where we stay more than a night or 2, we have a lot on our itinerary we want to do while we are in this area of Thailand.

Afterwards, we went back and got ready at the hostel to do a little hike. We wanted to go uptop this hill to get an entire view of Phuket province.

We started our walk at our hostel, and moved through the city, as we got closer to the hill, it got less and less touristy, until we stumbled upon a temple near by, with a giant golden Budha, we decided to inspect, see if there was much unique about the temple, but much seemed to be closed off for a school trip, so we just perservered.

Once we got to the hill, it was all nature and walk way, we admired the area and occasionally stopped at outposts setup near by to just take it light, till we got to the view point. Truly a sight to behold, and in the midst of a very hot thai day, the breeze there felt so nice. 

Around we also saw monkeys chilling and eating foods, there was supposedly a zone to go feed them in.

# Patong Beach

Still having plenty of day ahead of us, we decided to just call a grab from the view point to bring us all the way to Patong Beach, the closest west coast beach to us. During this season, it was abour 300 baht, so nearly 10 dollars usd, more expensive than the usual Grab but still cheaper than Uber.

\
Once we got there, we noticed immediately how many tourist traps there were, guys constantly trying to call us over to do jet skis, like every 2 minutes, having to pay for a chair to sit, and just generally a lot to try capitalize of tourist naivety/willingness to spend lots of cash on things(which like fair enough, just was a bit of a turn off for me and benji). We ended up getting a nice spot under a tree where Benji read his book while i went and to swim.

\
The water here was SOOOO nice, it was warmer, so i could just hop in, but still cooled me down. I swam outward for a bit, and just road the current. Whenever I was tired of swimming, i would just float along, and it felt so peaceful. 

after 2 hours there we headed back to Old town

# Chilva Market

For dinner, we ended up walking to Chilva market. It was very funny, while we were at the hostel, we were recommended to take a grab, and then Benji noted it was only 50 minute walk and I said “thats doable” and the hostel worker looked very shocked. 

This market was definitely one of the more grand night markets we had been too. For dinner I had halal thai takoyaki and egg wrapped chicken sausage, and benji had a mango salad. I got a Honey Lemon Soda as a drink as well!

\
On the walk home, we were minding out business till we passed this street dog, who got up and started following us, for a second we just thought he was just going in the same direction as us, but no, he just followed us for blocks. 

Benji kept on telling me I couldnt take him home as a pet, and thenhe proceeded to go into a 7/11 to go get dog food for him, so clearly he was charmed by him too. Sadly he ignored the food we got, either he is a picky eater or wasn’t hungry, but he did continue to follow us for a few more blocks.

\
he eventually went on his own way, dont know where that is, but he just seemed to be living his street dog life. That was about all that happened today, a little bit more of a relaxed day, but big things coming soon.
