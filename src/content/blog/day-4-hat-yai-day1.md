Hey everyone! Today marks day 2 of being in Thailand proper!

# \
Our Morning in Bangkok

Our day started at 6 am today, as our flight to Hat Yai was at 9:20am, and we also wanted to check out this morning market we had heard about the day prior. We got ready and dressed and we were out by 6:45. The hotel we stayed at was not far from DMK airport so we wandered through the market a tad. It was pretty much all fresh produce, meats, vegetables, and whatever people would need on a daily basis. All of it was from local farmers too! After wandering through the market, we got breakfast at a little stand, I just got a basic chicken on rice!

After wandering through a bit, we finally went into the airport, got hydration drinks(because we definitely need them; if you are ever in thailand, I recommend Pocari Sweat), and flew off to the start of our journey south to north: Hat Yai

# \
Early Day in Hat Yai

When we landed, we got a Grab(Thailand Uber), to our hostel to drop our bags off. We got there way before check in, so we first wandered around the area. In comparison to Bangkok where it was extremely packed and bustling, it was still bustling but felt a little less on the overwhelming side, and the area we are in felt a bit easier to walk. We ended up eating Lunch at a chinese restaurant, while discussing our plans for the rest of the day.

# The Plans for the Rest of the Day

So we went back to the hostel, where we had discovered that Benji has accidentally booked the female dormitory at the hostel, and had a good laugh with the lady managing the desk about it. She replaced the booking so we can be in the Male Dorms, we dropped our bags, and then headed off towards the Stainless steel temple.

This temple was gorgeous, looked nothing like any temple or religious structure we had ever seen before. Before we got in we made sure to review over temple etiquette: no shoes in the temple, our shirts must cover the shoulders, ensure our feet were not facing the budha statues, making sure we walk clockwise around the religious structures in the temple, and ensuring we know the proper greetings for monks. Monastic culture is very big here, so ensuring we knew the proper way to show respect was highly important(last thing we want to be is annoying tourists). The temple was beautiful having various paintings on the wall, beautiful architecture, and lots of nature around it. We didnt encounter any monks, but we did encounter some of the dogs they cared for. There is a culture here where abandoned dogs may be dropped off at temples to be left in the care of the monks, to give them a peaceful home. 

After the temple, we headed back to the hostel to rest for an hour before we heading back out to the Asean Night Market: a night market dedicated to all sorts of street clothes, wears and trinkets, as well as our dinner. The second floor of the market was just a food all and given that Hat Yai is known for their chicken, I got Chicken Satay from the market. We wandered the market for ages before saying we weren't that interested in anything and headed back to the hostel. The walk there and back was so nice and peaceful at times but somehow every other street would have a ton of Night life, which is not something we completely expected in cities outside of Bangkok, Phuket and Chiang Mai. 

# Overview

Overall, today was very active, lots of walking between places and activities(ended the day with 30k steps according to my phone), and plenty of opportunities to explore the markets scene in Hat Yai. We have another half day here in Hat Yai, so we are hoping to fit in a couple more things before we start heading towards Trang. Now, as I am still recovering from Jet Lag, I am preparing to go to sleep.
